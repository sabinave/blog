Lavalamp

After experimenting and looking closer at lava lamp implementations we have decided on implementing a lava lamp with somewhat reality-based functions.

Posted by Veronica Hage on Wednesday May, 2019

After experimenting and looking closer at lava lamp implementations we have decided on implementing a lava lamp with somewhat reality-based functions.

To do this we need to:

• Set a density to each blob

When the blob expands it will float upwards. When it reaches the top of the lava lamp it will then contract again, causing the density to increase and the blob will sink.

• Simulate a simplified heat emission from the bottom of the lamp.

The heat will have a fixed value at the bottom of the lamp that will decrease (preferably exponentially) when distance from it grows.

The heat will affect the blob’s density by simulating a volume expansion of the blob when closer to the bottom and according to the set temperature. If hot enough the radius of the blob will increase. The sought after effect: Our solution is simplified as in the real world fluid heating is done by convection which is more complicated than this.

Further steps:

• Apply randomness to somewhat simulate more realistically moving blobs.

• Add a GUI where the user can change parameters such as environmental density and heat.
